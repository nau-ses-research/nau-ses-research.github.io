Danielle Huntzinger

Danielle Huntzinger
Jan 7, 2025

About

Dr. Danielle Huntzinger is a Professor in Climate Sciences in the School of Earth and Sustainability at Northern Arizona University, where she has been a faculty member since 2011. She is the director of the Huntzinger Lab, which focuses on Carbon Cycle Science and Climate Change.

Research Interests

For almost a decade, Dr. Huntzinger’s research has been motivated by the desire to better understand the role of the natural and managed carbon cycle in climate change dynamics. Her research focuses on four main areas:

Diagnosis of the Terrestrial Carbon Cycle

  • Quantifying how much atmospheric carbon dioxide the land surface is taking up
  • Determining where carbon uptake is occurring geographically
  • Understanding how the land carbon budget has changed over time
  • Developing methods to monitor terrestrial carbon fluxes

Attribution of Carbon Cycle Changes

  • Identifying what processes drive changes in land-atmosphere carbon exchange
  • Understanding regional and global patterns in carbon cycle dynamics
  • Analyzing the role of natural vs. managed ecosystems in carbon cycling
  • Investigating climate drivers of carbon cycle variability

Future Carbon Cycle Projections

  • Projecting how the land carbon cycle will interact with Earth’s climate system
  • Understanding climate-carbon cycle feedbacks
  • Modeling future changes in terrestrial carbon storage
  • Assessing uncertainty in carbon cycle projections

Climate Science and Solutions

  • Integrating carbon cycle science with climate change mitigation strategies
  • Understanding the role of land management in carbon sequestration
  • Developing science-based solutions for climate challenges

Education

  • Ph.D. Environmental Science/Climate Dynamics, University of California, Irvine
  • M.S. Atmospheric Science, University of California, Davis
  • B.S. Environmental Science, University of California, Berkeley

Research Impact

Dr. Huntzinger’s work contributes directly to our understanding of how terrestrial ecosystems respond to and influence climate change, providing critical information for climate predictions and carbon management strategies.

Laboratory Focus

The Huntzinger Lab specializes in:

  • Carbon cycle modeling and data analysis
  • Integration of observational and modeling approaches
  • Multi-scale carbon cycle research from local to global
  • Collaborative research with national and international scientists

Graduate Student Supervision

Dr. Huntzinger mentors graduate students in:

  • Carbon cycle modeling techniques
  • Biogeochemical data analysis
  • Climate science research methods
  • Professional development in climate science

Collaborative Research

Dr. Huntzinger participates in:

  • National and international carbon cycle research networks
  • Multi-institutional climate modeling projects
  • Interdisciplinary climate change research initiatives
  • Collaborations with government agencies and research institutions

Professional Service

  • Contributing member of major carbon cycle research communities
  • Reviewer for climate science and biogeochemistry journals
  • Participant in national climate assessment activities
  • Advocate for science-based climate policy
